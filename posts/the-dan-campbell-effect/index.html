The Dan Campbell Effect

In 2008, the Detroit Lions ended their season with an 0-16 record, making them the first team in NFL history to finish a season with no wins. This sounds bad, and it is, but Detroit fans were all but used to it. The Lions organization originated in 1930 and were a decently successful team up until the super bowl era in 1966. During the super bowl era, up until 2020, the Lions had won 332 times compared to their 439 losses, while only winning one playoff game. Due to this, they were known as one of the worst franchises in all of sports.

But something changed in 2021 when the Lions hired Dan Campbell at Head Coach. Coach Campbell instantly drew attention from the media as in his opening press conference he famously said “We’re gonna kick you in the teeth, all right? And when you punch us back, we’re gonna smile at you, and when you knock us down, we’re going to get up. And on the way up, we’re going to bite a kneecap off.” This gave Lions fans a spark and a new confidence had grown in Detroit.

These graphs show the Lions offensive and defensive efficiency pre and post Dan Campbell. The difference is obvious and truly astounding. Before Campbell, the Lions were consistently one of the worst teams on offense and defense. After, it changed for the better. Every season under Campbell, the Lions improved on both sides of the ball, which ultimately led to more wins.

How exactly did this happen though. How was Campbell able to turn around a franchise that had only won one playoff game in 54 years. Diving deeper into how he was able to do this. The first thing to look at was the rushing attack, as Campbell wanted to begin his rebuild in the trenches.

These visuals show where Campbell started his rebuild. When Campbell first arrived in Detroit, he had two running backs in D’Andre Swift and Jamaal Williams. They were solid pieces for the growing offense but the team wanted more explosive plays. This led the Lions front office to let both running backs go, and picking up David Montgomery in free agency, along with Jahmyr Gibbs in the 2023 NFL draft. This was the explosiveness that the Lions were hungry for and it work out better than expected.

As you can see in the previous two graphs, the Lions rushing attack was improving with Campbell at head coach. But the jump from 2022-2024 is eye-opening. Using Montgomery as the power back and Gibbs as the speedy back, the Lions found a way to have the best rushing attack in the NFL.

What else could fuel an organization that has never felt the joy of winning seasons or the excitement of big games? Lets just say, Campbell brought a whole lot of grit and gutsy calls to the team and it helped. Since 2021, the Lions have the highest go-for-it rate in the NFL and that has led to some controversy of Campbell’s play calls. Has it worked or has it hurt the team? Let’s take a look.

Fourth downs make or break games for teams, especially for the Lions. At this point, it is a guarantee that if the Lions are past midfield and have less than three yards to go, Campbell is rolling the dice.

All in all, the Detroit Lions have had one of the most historic turnarounds in NFL history. Dan Campbell has proven that he is the guy for Detroit and is there to stay. Can Campbell bring a Lombardi trophy to Detroit or is the team still under the curse of Bobby Layne.
